The Difference

The audience comes for AI tools, then stays for the build.

Most AI tool videos feel disconnected: one app writes text, another makes a video, another builds a website, and then the video ends. This proposal keeps the strong “best tools” format, but adds a story underneath it.

The structure is simple: speak, learn, research, build, automate, distribute, and operate. The audience gets a list of powerful AI tools plus a live example of how those tools stack into a business.

Section 1

Reference Video Pattern

We borrow the clickability of “I tested 100+ AI tools” videos, but improve the execution so the demos build momentum.

Reference Video

Simon Squibb — “I Tried 500+ AI Tools, These 9 Will Make You Rich.”

This performed extremely well because the title is broad, clickable, and directly tied to making money. The video covered multiple useful categories: software building, AI video, voice, automation, meeting notes, speech-to-text, image generation, and chatbot comparisons. It had something for almost every online business owner.

Watch the reference video

Pros

  • Strong title with high-intent keywords.
  • Broad tool coverage across many business use cases.
  • Easy for non-technical founders to follow.
  • Clear commercial angle around AI tools and making money.

Cons

  • Tools felt like separate demos.
  • No strong connected storyline.
  • No clear build-up from one tool to the next.
  • Viewers learn tools, but do not see them stack into a full business system.
Our Upgrade

Keep the same clickable “AI tools that can make you money” angle, but make the video more coherent. Instead of random demos, every tool stacks into the next one as we build CookUp live.

Idea → Research → Build → Automate → Distribute → Operate

The format is familiar, clickable, and easy to understand. It promises the viewer a shortcut: “I tested the messy AI tool market, and these are the ones worth caring about.”

Most videos jump from tool to tool without a larger payoff: Tool 1 does this, Tool 2 does that, Tool 3 creates a video, Tool 4 builds a website. It is useful, but it can feel like a playlist of demos instead of a story.

Every tool still gets a clear standalone moment, but each tool also moves the same company forward. The video keeps the pacing of a tool roundup while gaining the satisfaction of a build challenge.

Section 2

Our Video: Build CookUp Live

CookUp is a simple home-chef meal ordering company built live using the AI stack.

Business We Build

CookUp

CookUp lets home chefs sell weekly homemade meals through a simple online menu, order form, and automated customer follow-up system.

“Let’s cook up a company live.”

Alternate line: “Let’s get cooking.”
01

Wispr Flow captures the idea fast.

Start with a messy spoken idea and turn it into clean business notes, prompts, product direction, and reusable copy. This gives the video a fast, relatable opening: before AI can build, we need a faster way to get ideas out.

Output: the first clean version of CookUp.
02

ChatGPT, Gemini, NotebookLM, Claude, and Grok shape the business.

Use research tools to inspect competitors, pricing, landing pages, examples, videos, notes, reports, and cultural trends. Claude turns the messy research into strategy, copy, a PRD, and build instructions.

Output: CookUp becomes a clear product direction with a build spec.
03

Lovable and n8n build and automate the product.

This is the first major deep dive. Lovable builds the product layer: home page, weekly menu, chef profiles, meal cards, preference forms, allergy inputs, order requests, confirmation page, and a simple admin dashboard.

n8n powers the backend: new orders are stored, AI summarizes the request, customers receive personalized follow-up, and chefs get clean prep summaries.

Output: a real software-enabled business, not just a landing page.
04

ElevenLabs and HeyGen create distribution.

Once CookUp exists, the video shifts to attention. ElevenLabs creates voiceovers, founder narration, chef voices, and audio assets. HeyGen creates chef intro videos, founder explainers, menu promos, ads, and short-form social assets.

Output: CookUp now has distribution assets for social media and the site.

Bonus: Operate the Company

This is the final-boss segment where the video becomes less about AI tools and more about an AI-powered operating system.

Capture

Granola

Captures chef onboarding calls, customer feedback, partnership conversations, and action items.

Remember

Obsidian

Stores chef notes, customer feedback, menu ideas, pricing notes, SOPs, launch checklists, and content ideas.

Operate

Reads business context, drafts follow-ups, organizes tasks, improves copy, updates plans, and helps run the company from the computer.

Final Section

Ending Story: Teach People How to Think With AI

The ending should make people feel like they did not just watch an AI tools video. They learned how to use AI as leverage.

We showed how to communicate with AI, which is basically prompt engineering. We also showed how to give AI the right notes, files, examples, and background, which is basically context engineering. Most people still do not understand these two things, so if the viewer gets this, they are already ahead of most people using AI.

The point is not that AI makes you instantly right. You still need human judgment. But with the right prompts, the right context, and human-in-the-loop thinking, it genuinely starts to feel like the Limitless movie effect.

Personal Angle

I had imposter syndrome working around extremely smart people, including PhDs from top universities. AI removed a lot of that intimidation because it helped me move into domains I did not come from.

Code

I cloned an open-source “zero-human company” style project and used Claude Code / Claude Cowork as a Q&A agent to understand the codebase and tinker with it quickly.

Real Estate

I also used AI this year to help work through a $10M commercial real estate deal at 26, even though I did not come from a commercial real estate background.

Final Message

I do not just want to show people tools. I want to teach them how to fish. Once you understand how to communicate with AI and give it the right context, you can use it to learn industries, build products, understand code, and operate way above your starting point.
