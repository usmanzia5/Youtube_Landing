What we are learning from Simon’s video

Reference Video

  • Broad tool coverage across many business use cases.
  • Easy for non-technical founders to follow.

✕ Cons

02 / Thought Process

The upgrade is the structure

The video should still feel like a high-performing AI tools roundup, but the tools need to stack into one clear build.

Core Angle

Each tool gets its own moment, but each tool also feeds into the next one. By the end, the audience should feel like we did not just test tools. We cooked up a company.

Business Built Live

CookUp is a simple home-chef meal ordering company. It has a weekly menu, chef profiles, order requests, automated customer follow-up, and a lightweight operating system behind it.

Audience Takeaway

The viewer gets both a list of powerful AI tools and a concrete example of how those tools can stack into a practical online business.

03 / YouTube Flow

Click through the video sections

This is the actual video layout. Each numbered bubble opens the part of the video, what happens there, and why it matters.

Part 1 / Input Layer

Capture the idea fast with Wispr Flow

Start with a messy spoken idea and turn it into clean business notes, prompts, product direction, saved snippets, and reusable copy. This is relatable because every founder has messy ideas; the tool makes the input layer faster.

Output: the first clean version of CookUp.

Part 2 / Learning + Strategy

Research and shape the business

ChatGPT Agent researches competitors, examples, pricing, landing pages, and business structure. Gemini / NotebookLM helps understand videos, PDFs, docs, notes, and reports. Claude turns the research into strategy, PRD, copy, and build instructions. Grok can help check internet-native language and trend framing.

Output: CookUp becomes a clear product direction with a build spec.

Part 3 / Build + Automate

Build the product with Lovable and n8n

Lovable builds the visible product layer: home page, weekly menu, chef profiles, meal cards, customer preference form, allergy and dietary inputs, order request flow, confirmation page, and a simple order dashboard.

n8n powers the backend workflow: new order comes in, order is stored in a sheet or CRM, AI summarizes the request, customer gets a personalized follow-up, and chef receives a prep summary.

Output: a real software-enabled business, not just a landing page.

Part 4 / Distribution

Create content assets with ElevenLabs and HeyGen

Once CookUp exists, the video shifts to attention. ElevenLabs creates founder narration, chef voices, voiceovers, and audio assets. HeyGen creates chef intro videos, founder explainers, weekly menu promos, ads, and short-form content.

Output: CookUp now has distribution assets for social media and the website.

Part 5 / Quick Promo

For e-commerce, mention Build Your Store

This is a quick mid-video note, not a full detour. If viewers are applying the same idea to e-commerce specifically, Build Your Store is the relevant shortcut because it is focused on getting an AI-powered online store moving quickly.

Visit Build Your Store

Part 6 / Operating System

Operate the company with Granola, Obsidian, and Claude Cowork

Granola captures chef calls, customer feedback, partnership conversations, and action items. Obsidian stores chef notes, customer feedback, menu ideas, pricing notes, SOPs, launch checklists, and content ideas. Claude Cowork uses that context to draft follow-ups, organize tasks, improve copy, update plans, and help manage the business.

Framing: Granola captures it. Obsidian remembers it. Claude Cowork operates it.

Part 7 / Ending Story

Close by teaching people how to think with AI

End by making people feel like they did not just watch an AI tools video. They learned how to communicate with AI, which is prompt engineering, and how to give AI the right notes, files, examples, and background, which is context engineering.

Connect it to the personal story: imposter syndrome around extremely smart people, using Claude Code / Claude Cowork to understand and tinker with a “zero-human company” codebase, and using AI to help work through a $10M commercial real estate deal at 26.

Final message: teach people how to fish, not just show them tools.
